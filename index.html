The Best Places I Visited in the Last Year

Documented by Abby Robins on January 28, 2025

Wilder Institute (The Calgary Zoo)

The Wilder Institute, formerly known as the Calgary Zoo, is full of excitement. Even in the winter, the institute is a great place to visit with friends and family. You will see foreign species, ranging from African giants to tiny Asian lizards. And, don't forget to check out the Canadian Wild! Here you can find wolves, buffalo, mountain goats, and of course the polar bear, Siku!

The penguins are adorable and you can get right up close to them either in their enclosure or on the amazing penguin walk! Even within the enclosure, there is a chance that a penguin will walk with you anyways, as some of them like to jump out to say hello!

Bowness Park

Bowness Park is a beautiful area to walk amongst nature and along the Bow River in Calgary.

Winter in Calgary can be a difficult time, however, Bowness Park offers a variety of activities in the winter as well! There are opportunities for skating around the pond, down the small river, and down an ice trail that runs along the Bow River. And, the park has numerous curling games that are free to use! Additionally, the trees throughout the park are wrapped in string lights that will uplift your experience. Enough about winter, here is a list of a few things you can find at Bowness Park during the summer season!

  • Large pond with friendly wildlife and amazing views
  • Walking and biking paths
  • Playgrounds and gathering spots with designated campfire/BQQ pits
  • Small train for young kids that loops around near the pond
  • Small watermark perfect for families with young kids
  • Serene restaurant that serves lunch and dinner, called Seasons of Bowness Park
  • Paddle boat rentals
  • Many other activities that everyone can enjoy!

Burnstick Lake

Burnstick Lake is a beautiful, but secluded interest point roughly 2 hours northwest of Calgary. It has a campground and a village with about 50 cabins. During the summer, visitors spend their time swimming in the lake, going for boat rides (including tubing, water skiing, wakeboarding, etc.), and of course, fishing.

In the winter, the lake freezes over and the community shovels ice rinks on the lake, as well as an amazing ice trial that goes out to the west point of the lake. Many owners of snow machines also bring them out to ride on the lake and on forest trails. It is the perfect weekend getaway from the city.
